TITLE OF RESEARCH PROJECT

Adaptive Testing of NLP models
Principal Investigator:
Marco Tulio Ribeiro, Scott Lundberg

PURPOSE

The purpose of this project is to compare different methodologies for testing natural language processing models.

PROCEDURES

During this project, the following will happen: we will give you access to a NLP model and ask you to engage in ‘red teaming’: coming up with input/output pairs that cause the model to fail in specific ways. You will have access to interactive visualizations and assistants that will suggest ‘test cases’. At the end of the study, you will be asked to evaluate whether the tools provided are helpful. The expected duration of the experiment 40 minutes. You will work on your own computer, and you will need access to the internet.
Microsoft may document and collect information about your participation by how you interact with the visualization. Researchers will have access to the examples you create, as well as your visualization assessments, which may be mentioned in a research paper (either in aggregate or individual test examples) or published as supplementary material. Approximately 10-50 participants will be involved in this study.

BENEFITS AND RISKS

Benefits: There are no direct benefits to you that might reasonably be expected as a result of being in this study. The research team expects to learn the impact of different red-teaming strategies from the results of this research, as well as any public benefit that may come these Research Results being shared with the greater scientific community.

Risks: During your participation, you may be exposed to strong language in automatically generated suggestions of test cases, including curse words, profanity, remarks that propagate harmful stereotypes, etc. If you are uncomfortable, you are free to stop your participation at any time.
